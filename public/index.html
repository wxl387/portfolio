About

Greetings! My name is Wenbiao Li and I am a graduate student of the Case Western Reserve University of Civil Engineering and certified Web Developer from the CWRU Coding Bootcamp.

This is my story. While studying as an undergrad, I developed growing interests in math, logic, and computer science that has since turned into a full-blown passion. After years of education in science and engineering, I decided to take the plunge and immerse myself in programming. As a person who lives and breathes data, I strive to layer my newfound software skillset on top of my science and engineering background to develop business intelligence applications that measure, track, and improve productivity.

The specific software development skills in my toolbelt include: JavaScript, jQuery, NodeJS, Express, ReactJS, HTML5/CSS3, Bootstrap, Materialize, Git, Responsive Design, API/JSON, RESTful API, Firebase, MySQL, MongoDB, AJAX, and Database Modeling.

In addition to fullstack web development skills, I have programming experience in Java, Python, Matlab, Excel VBA, and familiarity with C and C++. Please check out some of my projects in the portfolio section below! Note that I will continue to add new passion projects as they are completed, so please be sure to come back in the future to see how my portfolio progresses!

Eat Da Burger

A Node, Express, Handlebars, and MySQL burger app that lets users input the names of burgers they'd like to eat... and then devour them. Using an home-grown ORM, the app queries MySQL to retrieve all entries and allows users to update or create burgers. The app also has a funny "Whine" feature, where random sound effects are played to grieve a devoured burger. The whine feature uses jQuery click listeners.

Eat Da Burger II

A Node, Express, Handlebars, and MySQL burger app that lets users input the names of burgers they'd like to eat. This app is a remake of my original Eat Da Burger application. The key difference is that it uses the Sequelize ORM instead of raw MySQL queries. Using Sequelize, the app is now relational, tracking which users ate which burger.

News Paper, Web Scraper

A Node.js and MongoDB application that web-scrapes news data from The Onion and allows users to comment about what they have read. Users can also delete unwanted comments. On the Back End, the app uses Express to serve routes and Mongoose to interact with a MongoDB database. On the Front End, the app uses Handlebars for templating each article and Materialize as a styling framework. The app also uses jQuery and AJAX to help with making post requests. And for webscraping, the app uses the request and cheerio Node packages.
